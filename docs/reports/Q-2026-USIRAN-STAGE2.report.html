Open assumptions — review before solving

  • Actor positions were mapped onto the 0-100 continuum by the formalizer.
    Sources describe qualitative negotiating stances but assign no numeric location on the settlement continuum.
  • Capability values (US=100 anchor; Iran ~68; Israel ~58; blocs lower) are formalizer estimates.
    Sources describe force posture and leverage qualitatively but give no comparable capability metric.
  • Salience values are formalizer estimates.
    Sources indicate which actors treat the issue as existential but provide no measured salience.
  • Gulf states were split into a 'UAE hawkish' bloc and a 'moderate Gulf (Oman/Qatar/Saudi)' bloc.
    Notes permit bloc aggregation; the underlying split is documented but the specific groupings and their single positions are formalizer constructs.
  • China and Pakistan aggregated into one mediation bloc.
    They issued a joint plan and Pakistan mediates, but treating them as a single actor with one position/capability is a formalizer simplification.
  • IAEA included as a distinct verification-oriented actor with an intermediate position.
    Sources cite Grossi's stance on inspections but do not characterise the IAEA as a bargaining participant with a settlement position.
  • Interpreting the US 'mode' as ~22 (not 0) reflects reported willingness to entertain a ~20-year moratorium.
    Sources note a reported bridging proposal but do not state an official softening of the zero-enrichment position.
  • Horizon treated as repeated/staged bargaining.
    The MOU's three-stage structure implies iterated interaction, but the sources do not formally characterise the game's temporal structure.
