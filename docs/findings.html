The method, and its negative results

Findings

A fair, pre-registered evaluation of the reconstructed model — reported with the same prominence whether it passes or fails. It fails, and that is the finding.

Pre-registered gates & verdicts

  • Gate v2 — fair fight. The fully-equipped challenge solver must beat the equally-equipped weighted mean on DEU MAE. Verdict: FAILED (challenge 26.83 / 38.51 vs mean 22.99 / 29.77; median baseline 28.37 / 40.64).
  • Successor gate. Each candidate must beat the compromise mean on the untouched TEST split. Verdict: no candidate beats the mean.
  • Ceiling diagnostic. A flexible cross-validated oracle scores 23.84 vs 22.99; the gap to the mean is -0.84 — at or below zero places the mean at the extractable-signal ceiling. cooperative domain only

The ceiling

The oracle model — deliberately flexible, cross-validated — scores 23.84 vs 22.99 against the compromise mean, a gap of -0.84. Even an optimistic flexible model does not beat the mean: there is essentially no signal beyond the influence-weighted average, which is why every model tried fails to beat it.
