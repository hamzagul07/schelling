An open, continuously-audited forecasting engine

Structure, not magic.

A bounded geopolitical question becomes a formal bargaining game, a deterministic solution, and a probability forecast — with a complete audit trail.

Every probability is computed, never guessed by a model. Every run is seeded and reproducible. Every live forecast is sealed before the event resolves.

The finding, in three sentences

On 351 expert-coded EU legislative decisions, the reconstructed Bueno de Mesquita challenge model scores a mean absolute error of 26.83 and loses a pre-registered accuracy gate to a far simpler benchmark — the capability-and-salience-weighted mean, at 22.99. A deliberately flexible cross-validated oracle then posts a ceiling gap of -0.84 against that mean: at or below zero, the weighted mean already sits at the extractable-signal ceiling for this domain and input set. The machinery was not beaten by a better model; there was no further signal to extract — structure, not magic.

Four movements

  1. Replication
    Rebuild the model from the Scholz-Calbert-Smith reconstruction with every interpretive choice logged, and reproduce the emission case at 9.530.
  2. The fair fight
    Score it on 351 expert-coded EU decisions with sourced capabilities and reference points against a gate fixed in advance — it fails (FAILED).
  3. The successor search
    Commit the train/dev/test split to version control before any fitting; two structurally-motivated successors both fail on the untouched test split.
  4. The ceiling
    A flexible oracle shows the simple mean is already at the extractable-signal ceiling (-0.84). Then seal live forecasts, cryptographically, before events resolve.

How to verify

Anyone can audit a sealed forecast without trusting us. The record files are gitignored (commit-reveal), so no number can be edited after the fact; reveal and check locally:

# recompute a sealed record's hash and re-solve it deterministically
schelling verify runs/<record>.json

# or, by hand, and match the digest against the ledger row
sha256sum runs/<record>.json

Full verification — including the OpenTimestamps Bitcoin anchor — is on the ledger page. The method and its negative results are on findings.
